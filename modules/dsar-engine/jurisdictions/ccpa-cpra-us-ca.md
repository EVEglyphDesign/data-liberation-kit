# Jurisdiction: CCPA / CPRA (California, USA)

**Statute:** California Consumer Privacy Act as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act (Cal. Civ. Code §1798.100 et seq.)
**Right:** Right to Know / Access — §1798.100, §1798.110, §1798.115
**Regulator:** California Privacy Protection Agency (CPPA) + California Attorney General.

## Response clock

- **45 days** to respond to a verifiable consumer request.
- Extension: **one additional 45-day period** when reasonably necessary, with notice to the consumer within the first 45 days.
- Businesses must acknowledge receipt within 10 business days and describe how the request will be processed.
- Source: [Terms.law — CCPA/CPRA privacy rights](https://terms.law/2025/12/04/ccpa-cpra-privacy-rights-demand-letters/)

## Scope

- Categories and specific pieces of PI collected, sources, business/commercial purposes, categories of third parties shared with/sold to.
- Lookback: 12 months minimum; CPRA extends to data collected on/after Jan 1, 2022 on request.

## Cost

- Free; up to twice per 12-month period per consumer.

## Escalation path (maps to DSAR ladder)

- **R2 non-compliance:** 45-day (or extended 90-day) deadline passes without adequate response.
- **R3 regulator:** complaint to the **CPPA** and/or California **AG**.
- Note: other US state laws (Virginia VCDPA, Colorado CPA, Connecticut, Utah, Texas, etc.) have their own clocks — add as sibling files when a class member is in-state.

## Clock formula

`deadline = sent_date + 45 days` · `extended_deadline = sent_date + 90 days (with notice in first 45 days)`

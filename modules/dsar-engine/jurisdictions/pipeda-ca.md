# Jurisdiction: PIPEDA (Canada)

**Statute:** Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA)
**Right:** Right of access — Principle 4.9 (Schedule 1) / s.8
**Regulator:** Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada (OPC) — priv.gc.ca

## Response clock

- **30 days** from receipt of the request.
- Extension: **up to 30 additional days** permitted where responding within 30 days would unreasonably interfere with the organization's activities, or time is needed for consultation/conversion — the organization must **notify the individual within the original 30 days** of the extension and its length.
- Source: [OPC — Access to personal information](https://www.priv.gc.ca/en/privacy-topics/privacy-laws-in-canada/the-personal-information-protection-and-electronic-documents-act-pipeda/p_principle/principles/p_access/)

## Cost

- Access must generally be provided **at minimal or no cost** to the individual.

## Required elements of a valid request

- Individual identifies themselves (identity verification permitted).
- Requests access to all personal information held, its uses, and third parties to whom it has been disclosed.

## Escalation path (maps to DSAR ladder)

- **R2 non-compliance:** deadline (30d, or extended 60d with notice) passes without adequate response.
- **R3 regulator:** file a complaint with the **OPC**. PIPEDA complaints must generally be filed within a set period of becoming aware of the issue.
- Note: Provincial private-sector laws (Quebec Law 25, BC PIPA, Alberta PIPA) may apply instead of PIPEDA for provincially-regulated organizations — flag per platform.

## Clock formula

`deadline = sent_date + 30 days` · `extended_deadline = sent_date + 60 days (only if extension notice received within 30 days)`
